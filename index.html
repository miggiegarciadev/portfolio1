About Me

About Me

My name is Miggie Garcia (it rhymes with Biggie [Smalls] the rapper) and I am a proud Afro-Latina femme. Currently, I am Full-Stack Software Engineer with Resilient Coders. I am an advocate for social justice; I believe in achieving equity through a means of knowledge of self and reciprocity within the Black community. I am a strong supporter of dismantling the school-to-prison pipeline as I have seen first hand how it can destroy a community and local families. I came of age in an urban place known as the concrete jungle. This is when I developed the resiliency to bounce back in the toughest of situations, but always keeping a positive and clear mind.

I pride myself in having a great work ethic and a passion for getting things done. I learned all about working hard from my grandma who did not speak any English. She helped to raise my multi-generational family while working in a tie factory as an immigrant. At 19 years old, I made the decision to enlist in the U.S. Army because I was in search of community and looking for a way out of the â€˜hood. I served for almost ten years with one tour to Kuwait, under Operation Enduring Freedom, as a Financial Management Technician. It was during my tenure in the Army that I learned about discipline, determination, and leadership. These values are very important to me because I approach everything in life this way. It takes determination to have discipline and it takes a true warrior to be able to lead even when things do not go according to plan.

I am my Ancestors' wildest dreams!
